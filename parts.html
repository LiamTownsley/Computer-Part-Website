Random Access Memory
RAM stands for Random Access Memory, and it's a type of fast storage that your computer uses to hold data it needs in the short term and can be accessed almost instantly. RAM has a very fast path to the computer's Central Processing Unit, or CPU, essentially the brain of the computer. Think of your hard drive as a filing cabinet where all your data is stored and the RAM is like your desk, where you put the stuff you're currently working on.		 Computer Case
The computer case serves mainly as a way to physically mount, contain and protect all of the actual components inside a computer, like the motherboard, hard drive, optical drive, etc.
Solid State Drive
A solid-state drive, or SSD, is a storage device used in computers. SSDs replace mechanical hard disks by using flash-based memory, which is significantly faster. SSDs speed up computers significantly due to their low read-access times and fast throughputs. The benefits of Solid state drives, they deliver faster load times for games, applications, and movies, and because of the technology they use, SSDs are lighter and better able to withstand movement and droppage. In addition, solid state drives use less energy, allowing computers to run cooler.		 If you would like to contribute to this, you can contact us.
